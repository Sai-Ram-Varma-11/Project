Featured Endangered Species

Amur Leopard

The Amur leopard is a critically endangered big cat found in the temperate forests of the Russian Far East and northeast China.

Population: Approximately 100 individuals remain in the wild.

Threats: Habitat loss, poaching, and prey depletion.

Sumatran Orangutan

Native to Sumatra, Indonesia, the Sumatran orangutan is classified as critically endangered due to habitat destruction.

Population: About 14,000 individuals left in the wild.

Threats: Deforestation and illegal pet trade.

Vaquita

The Vaquita is the world's rarest marine mammal, found only in the northern part of the Gulf of California, Mexico.

Population: Fewer than 30 individuals remain.

Threats: Bycatch in illegal gillnets used for fishing.
